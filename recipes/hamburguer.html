Hamburguer

Description

A well known burguer in bread. A recipe that everyone eats and everybody knows how to make.

The amazing part about it, is the infinite combinations that can be make with it.

Ingredients

The bread is by choice, i used the supermarket hambuerguer specific bread.

For the burguer:

  • Ground chuck - I put a preference of an 80 lean/20 fat ratio
  • Crushed crackers or bread crumbs (can be gluten free)
  • Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 Egg
  • Milk
  • Seasoning: salt, garlic powder, pepper or other spices

Steps

  1. Set out a large mixing bowl and add in the ground beed, crushed crackers, egg, worcestershire sauce, milk and spices.
  2. Use your hands to thoroughly combine until the mixture is very smooth.
  3. Press the mear down in the bowl, into an even disk. Now use a knife to cut and divide the hamburguer patty mixture into a 6-1/3 pound frill or skillet patties, or 12 thin griddle patties.
  4. Set out a baking sheet, lined with wax paper or foil, to hold the patties.
  5. One at a time, gather the patty mix and press firmly into patties of your desire thickness.
  6. Place the formed patties on the baking sheet. With thick patties press a dent in the center of each patty, so they don't puff up while cooking.
  7. Preheat the grill or a skillet to medium heat, approximately 180-220 degrees C.
  8. Grill for 2 minutes if you want thin patties or 3-4 minutes for thicker patties per side.
  9. Now prepare the buns and everything you want in it and it's ready to serve and eat.
